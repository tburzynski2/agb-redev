Permits for Building a Garage in Wisconsin

8 Steps to Secure Your Permit

Garage permits in Wisconsin are typically required for both attached and detached garages. The specific requirements vary by municipality, but the general guidelines include:

  1. Permit Application: Submit a building permit application to your local municipality. Many cities offer online permit systems.
  2. Site Plan: Include a site plan showing the proposed garage location, property lines, and setbacks.
  3. Building Plans: Submit detailed building plans or pre-fab structure information with dimensions, materials, and structural details.
  4. Zoning Compliance: Ensure your plans comply with local zoning regulations, including setbacks and size restrictions.
  5. Contractor Information: Provide contractor credentials if applicable. Attached garages require state certification.
  6. Electrical Permits: Obtain separate electrical permits if installing electrical systems.
  7. Fees: Permit fees vary by municipality and project scope.
  8. Inspections: Schedule required inspections, including a final inspection.

The Wisconsin Department of Safety and Professional Services oversees the Uniform Dwelling Code, which applies to one- and two-family dwellings, including attached garages.

When you work with American Garage Builders, we handle all permits, zoning, and building processes on your behalf.
